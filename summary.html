Analysis

Initial exploratory analysis showed that proportions of respondents to each question are reasonably in line with expected population proportions. For example, we would expect that 89% of Canadians have a High School diploma or equivalent, with about 57% also having some form of post secondary certification. The actual values show approximately 60% of respondents have a post secondary certification, with an additional 25% having high school equivalent, and about 15% with no certification.

The Data

This data is collected from the Canadian Social Survey, with data from 2022, 2023, and 2024. Data for institutional confidence was collected twice per year for each of these years.

The CSS is intended to provide information to the government on developing social issues in Canada. The survey is limited to the 10 provinces, and to persons not living on a reserve. Non-institutionalized persons, age 15 or older are eligible, but the survey is available only in English and French.

The variables examined here are a small subset of all data collected in the CSS. The Confidence indicators were not included in all iterations of the CSS, which is administered quarterly.
